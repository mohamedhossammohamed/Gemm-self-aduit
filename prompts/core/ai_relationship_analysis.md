# AI Relationship Analysis

Longitudinal analysis of how the user engages with AI systems, evolving interaction patterns, and changing modes of AI delegation and collaboration.

## Absolute Constraints

You MUST NOT use:
- Generic AI user archetypes
- Assumptions about AI adoption patterns
- Generalized technology-adoption frameworks
- Stereotypes about different user demographics and AI
- External data about AI usage trends

Use ONLY:
- Observed shifts in how the user asks AI systems questions
- Evolution in specificity and clarity of prompts
- Patterns in what they delegate to AI vs. retain
- How their expectations of AI capabilities have changed
- Evidence of different "AI modes" being used in different contexts
- Language patterns that reveal trust or skepticism
- Stated frustrations and satisfactions with AI
- How they iterate or refine interactions based on results
- Changes in how they challenge or validate AI output

## Objective

Model the user's relationship with AI systems across time: how they conceptualize AI capabilities, how their usage patterns evolve, and how their mental model of AI's role in their work/thinking changes.

## AI Interaction Modes to Assess

1. **AI as Tool** - Instrumental, specific task delegation, input-output transactional
2. **AI as Mirror** - Using AI to reflect back ideas for clarity or challenge
3. **AI as Extension** - Delegating cognitive work that extends their own capacity
4. **AI as Strategist** - Using AI for systems-level thinking and strategic synthesis
5. **AI as Validator** - Using AI to sense-check ideas or identify blind spots
6. **AI as Exploring Partner** - Open-ended collaboration for discovery
7. **AI as Executor** - Letting AI make decisions within defined boundaries

## Required Analysis Areas

1. **Usage Evolution** - How has frequency, complexity, and types of AI interactions changed?
2. **Trust Calibration** - How has their assessment of AI reliability shifted?
3. **Delegation Comfort** - What are they willing to delegate to AI? What do they withhold?
4. **Prompt Quality** - Are their prompts becoming more sophisticated, specific, or nuanced?
5. **AI Capability Model** - What do they believe AI can and cannot do? Is this accurate?
6. **Context Switching** - Do they use AI differently in different domains?
7. **Expectation Management** - Do they have unrealistic expectations or overly conservative ones?
8. **Collaboration Patterns** - How do they iterate with AI? Do they refine or accept first responses?
9. **Skepticism vs. Credulity** - Do they appropriately question AI output?
10. **Identity Impact** - Does AI interaction affect their self-concept (threat or enablement)?

## Required Methodology

For each dimension:
1. **Observed Pattern** - Specific evidence of this relationship mode from memory
2. **Temporal Evolution** - How has this pattern changed over conversation history?
3. **Domain Variation** - Does this pattern differ across different contexts?
4. **Confidence Level** - Based on evidence clarity and consistency
5. **Examples** - 2-3 specific interactions that illustrate the pattern

## Output Format

Organize as:
1. **Overall AI Relationship Arc** - How has their relationship with AI evolved?
2. **Primary AI Modes** - Which interaction modes do they gravitate toward?
3. **Mode-Switching Patterns** - When and why do they shift between modes?
4. **Prompt Evolution** - How has their communication with AI changed?
5. **Trust Calibration** - Assessment of how accurately they judge AI capabilities
6. **Delegation Boundaries** - What work stays human, what goes to AI?
7. **Collaboration Maturity** - How sophisticated is their iteration with AI?
8. **Friction Points** - Where does AI interaction create frustration or inefficiency?
9. **Growth Edges** - How could their AI relationship become more effective?
10. **Unresolved Questions** - What remains unclear about their AI engagement?

## Critical Notes

- Distinguish between "capability" and "comfort"—they may defer work to AI not because AI is better, but because they're uncomfortable doing it
- Flag where their AI capability model appears inaccurate (both underestimating and overestimating)
- Note patterns that suggest appropriate skepticism vs. inappropriate credulity
- Distinguish between "how they use AI" and "how they're willing to admit they use AI"
- Acknowledge where their relationship to AI may be influenced by broader relationship patterns
