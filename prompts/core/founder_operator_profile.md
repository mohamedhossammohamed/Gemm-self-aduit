# Founder/Operator Profile

Analysis of leadership patterns, execution philosophy, organizational instincts, and strategic decision-making style based exclusively on observed behavior and stated approaches.

## Absolute Constraints

You MUST NOT use:
- Leadership personality stereotypes
- Founder "type" classifications
- Business school frameworks applied prescriptively
- Assumed growth ambition patterns
- External benchmarking against successful founders
- Generic CEO or operator archetypes

Use ONLY:
- How the user describes organizational decisions they've made or would make
- Patterns in how they approach problems requiring execution
- Stated values around leadership and how they align with observed choices
- Their relationship to delegation, control, and trust
- How they handle team dynamics or organizational friction
- Risk tolerance expressed in operational decisions
- Resource allocation priorities
- How they measure success or failure in operational contexts

## Objective

Model the user's operational instincts and leadership philosophy: how they naturally approach building, organizing, executing, and scaling.

This is NOT normative assessment of leadership quality. It is architectural understanding of their operational mind.

## Required Analysis Areas

1. **Decision-Making Philosophy** - Centralized or distributed? Fast or consensus-driven? Data or intuition-based?
2. **Execution Style** - Plan-then-execute or iterate-and-learn? Structured or organic?
3. **Risk Tolerance** - How do they relate to organizational uncertainty and failure?
4. **Delegation Instincts** - Do they naturally trust others? Retain control? Empower?
5. **Organizational Values** - What do they prioritize (speed, quality, relationships, impact)?
6. **Team Dynamics** - How do they think about and handle group dynamics and conflict?
7. **Scaling Philosophy** - How do they think about growth? What concerns them about scale?
8. **Resource Prioritization** - What gets funded first, second, last?
9. **Feedback Reception** - How do they incorporate criticism or data that challenges plans?
10. **Strategic vs. Tactical** - Do they naturally focus on long-term or immediate execution?

## Required Methodology

For each operational dimension:
1. **Stated Approach** - How does the user describe their leadership/operational style?
2. **Observed Pattern** - What actual choices or behaviors align with or contradict this?
3. **Specific Examples** - Decisions or situations that illustrate this pattern
4. **Confidence Level** - Based on consistency and evidence across memory
5. **Evolution** - Has their operational approach shifted or matured over time?
6. **Tensions** - Where do stated values conflict with observed choices?

## Output Format

Organize as:
1. **Operational Profile Summary** - Overall leadership/operator philosophy
2. **Core Decision-Making Patterns** - How they naturally approach choices
3. **Organizational Instincts** - What they naturally gravitate toward in structure and culture
4. **Execution Strengths** - Where their operational approach is strongest
5. **Execution Vulnerabilities** - Where their natural approach may create problems
6. **Team and People Dynamics** - How they relate to human organization
7. **Scaling Readiness** - How their instincts change as scope increases
8. **Value-Action Alignment** - Do stated operational values match actual choices?
9. **Growth Philosophy** - How they think about evolution and change
10. **Unresolved Questions** - What remains unclear about their operational mind?

## Critical Notes

- Distinguish between "how they've operated" and "how they would operate" (are these consistent?)
- Flag where constraints (resources, team, market) may mask their true operational instincts
- Note how their thinking about operations may be aspirational vs. actual
- Avoid assuming founder identity—model operator patterns even if they haven't built organizations
- Acknowledge where insufficient operational context exists in memory
