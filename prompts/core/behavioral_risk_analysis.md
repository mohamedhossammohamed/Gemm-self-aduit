# Behavioral Risk Analysis

Deep analysis of psychological and operational risk patterns with mandatory uncertainty boundaries. Identifies burnout vectors, optimization traps, cognitive failure modes, and behavioral volatility based exclusively on persistent memory evidence.

## Absolute Constraints

You MUST NOT use:
- Generic psychological risk profiles
- Personality-type risk stereotypes
- Population-based vulnerability statistics
- External diagnostic criteria
- Psychopathological terminology without evidence
- Generalized stress-response models

Use ONLY:
- Observed behavioral patterns across documented situations
- Repeated cycles or escalation patterns in memory
- Stated stressors and observed coping mechanisms
- Decision-making deterioration under specific conditions
- Self-reported vulnerability signals in language
- Intensity escalation in work/thought patterns
- Contradictions between values and execution that create friction

## Objective

Identify and model specific risk vectors that could lead to performance degradation, decision failure, psychological strain, or operational breakdown, grounded entirely in observable patterns.

This is NOT pathologization, therapy, or prescription. It is risk recognition.

## Required Analysis Areas

1. **Over-Optimization Risk** - Over-systematization, perfectionism, unsustainable pace
2. **Burnout Vectors** - Patterns that escalate unsustainably (work intensity, cognitive load, emotional labor)
3. **Ideation Loops** - Recursive thinking that may lack external reality-check (rumination, planning spirals)
4. **Decision-Making Failure Modes** - Conditions that degrade judgment (fatigue, emotional charge, time pressure, insufficient information)
5. **Cognitive Rigidity Risks** - Attachment to frameworks that may become obsolete
6. **Relationship Volatility** - Patterns in AI/human relationships that create instability
7. **Value-Action Misalignment Strain** - Friction from stated values conflicting with actual behavior
8. **Recovery Capacity** - Ability to recognize and exit unsustainable patterns

## Required Methodology

For each identified risk:
1. **Observed Pattern** - Specific evidence from memory showing this risk
2. **Triggering Conditions** - What situations or states activate this risk?
3. **Escalation Path** - How does this risk progress over time?
4. **Current Severity** - Assessment based on frequency and intensity in memory
5. **Confidence Level** - low, moderate, or high (based on evidence quantity)
6. **Mitigation Factors** - What in the user's patterns prevents full escalation?
7. **Early Warning Signals** - Behavioral markers that would indicate risk increasing

## Output Format

Organize as:
1. **Risk Severity Map** - Matrix of identified risks by likelihood and impact
2. **High-Confidence Risk Vectors** - Well-evidenced patterns with specific examples
3. **Moderate-Evidence Risks** - Patterns observed but not yet fully clear
4. **Speculative Risk Hypotheses** - Patterns that could exist but lack sufficient evidence
5. **Protective Factors** - Strengths and mechanisms that mitigate risk
6. **Risk Interactions** - How different risks compound or interact with each other
7. **Intervention Points** - Where intervention could be highest-leverage
8. **Unresolved Ambiguities** - What additional evidence would clarify risk assessment?

## Critical Tagging Requirements

- Mark all inferences with explicit confidence tags: low, moderate, or high; label speculation separately as speculative
- Distinguish between "pattern observed in memory" vs. "pattern likely to intensify"
- Never assume mental health diagnosis from behavioral patterns
- Acknowledge where insufficient data exists to make risk judgments
- Flag where your assessment might be influenced by projection bias
