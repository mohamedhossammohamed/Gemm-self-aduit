# Cognitive Architecture Analysis

Deep examination of the user's thinking patterns, reasoning frameworks, problem-solving approaches, and decision-making style based exclusively on conversational and behavioral evidence.

## Absolute Constraints

You MUST NOT use:
- IQ or cognitive ability testing frameworks
- Standardized personality-thinking-style inventories
- Population-based cognitive norms
- Neuroscientific assumptions without behavioral evidence
- Generic "thinking type" categories
- Assumed learning style preferences

Use ONLY:
- Observable reasoning patterns in explanations and questions
- How problems are decomposed and approached
- What information the user seeks or ignores
- How they handle ambiguity, contradiction, or uncertainty
- Their speed and depth of pattern recognition
- How they revise thinking when challenged
- Domain-specific vs. abstract reasoning patterns
- How they balance theory and empiricism

## Objective

Model the user's cognitive operating system: their natural reasoning approaches, frameworks they default to, edge cases they handle well or poorly, and the evolution of their thinking sophistication.

This is structural analysis of thought, not evaluation of thinking quality.

## Required Analysis Areas

1. **Abstraction Capacity** - How high can they generalize? Do they prefer concrete or abstract models?
2. **Systems Reasoning** - How well do they model complex interdependencies and feedback loops?
3. **Ambiguity Tolerance** - How do they function with incomplete or contradictory information?
4. **Pattern Recognition Style** - Do they pattern-match rapidly? Do they seek exceptions?
5. **Optimization Orientation** - Do they naturally optimize for efficiency, safety, elegance, etc.?
6. **Epistemological Framework** - How do they distinguish between evidence, interpretation, and speculation?
7. **Tool Relationship** - How do they integrate new tools (frameworks, languages, systems)?
8. **Contradiction Resolution** - How do they handle internal logical conflicts?
9. **Reasoning Depth vs. Speed** - Do they prioritize thoroughness or velocity?
10. **Domain Expertise vs. Transfer** - What domains show depth? How do they apply knowledge outside domains?

## Required Methodology

For each cognitive dimension:
1. **Observed Pattern** - Specific evidence of this reasoning mode from memory
2. **Examples** - 2-3 concrete instances where this pattern appeared
3. **Constraints** - What conditions activate or suppress this pattern?
4. **Confidence Level** - Based on frequency and clarity of evidence
5. **Evolution** - Has this pattern strengthened, weakened, or become more nuanced over time?
6. **Complementary Patterns** - What other cognitive modes does this interact with?

## Output Format

Organize as:
1. **Cognitive Profile Summary** - Overall reasoning style in paragraph form
2. **Core Reasoning Frameworks** - Primary approaches they default to
3. **Flexibility Points** - Where thinking is adaptive vs. rigid
4. **Blind Spots** - Where reasoning appears to skip steps or ignore evidence
5. **Strength Areas** - Domains or problem types where cognition is strongest
6. **Challenge Areas** - Where reasoning struggles or works less smoothly
7. **Evolution Trajectory** - How has their thinking changed over the conversation history?
8. **Integration with AI Systems** - How they reason about and delegate to AI tools
9. **Unresolved Questions** - What cognitive patterns remain unclear?

## Validation Notes

- Distinguish between "how they reason" and "what they know"
- Flag where apparent cognitive constraints might be knowledge gaps instead
- Note when stress, time pressure, or emotional state may be affecting observed patterns
- Avoid assuming consistency—humans are context-dependent; note when their reasoning shifts
- Distinguish between cognitive capacity and cognitive preference
